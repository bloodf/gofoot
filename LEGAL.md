## 1. No login. Session-token identity.

GoFoot does **not** use email/password, OAuth, or magic links. A user is identified solely
by a cryptographically random 256-bit **session token** that the user mints on first visit
and is responsible for keeping safe. Losing the token = losing the save (TursoDB has no
recovery flow, by design — there is no account to recover, no email to look up).

The token is HMAC-signed (`sessionSecret`) so it cannot be forged. The server cannot help
you recover a lost token because the server has no email or other identifier attached to it.

## 2. Server-side authority.

**The client is untrusted.** Match outcomes, transfer negotiations, sponsorship deals,
youth intake, cup progression, financial settlements, and reputation unlocks are all
computed on the **TursoDB-backed Nitro server**. The client only receives **signed snapshots
(HMAC-chained)** that it can render. There is no client endpoint that lets the user edit
cash, attributes, or squads directly.

Every state mutation is recorded in an **audit log** whose entries form a hash chain
authenticated with the server's HMAC secret. A user can verify the integrity of their own
chain at `/audit` at any time. If the chain breaks, the UI stops trusting new snapshots and
refuses to advance game time.

## 3. Real player / club / competition names.

Player names, club names, competition names, and factual match data are sourced from public
APIs and community mirror datasets (API-Football, football-data.org, TheSportsDB,
OpenFootball, FBref, Wikipedia, Wikimedia Commons) and are used for factual, non-commercial,
open-source purposes consistent with nominative fair use. They are not used to imply any
endorsement or affiliation.

## 4. Sponsors — 100% fictional Brazilian meme brands.

**No real commercial brand appears in any sponsor slot in GoFoot, ever.** Sponsors are drawn
exclusively from `data/joke-brands.json` — a registry of fictional, parody names rooted in
Brazilian meme culture and classic TV advertising tropes (e.g. *"Indústrias Tabajara"*,
*"Água em Pó Birigui"*, *"Apostano"*, *"Casas Bacia"*, *"Sabonete Cacique Indalino"*,
*"Geladeira do Posto Soares"*). These parodies are not meant to disparage any real brand,
present party, product, or service; they exist purely for nostalgic-comedic effect in a
fictional sponsorship system. **No real-world brand** is referenced in code, JSON, locale
files, comments, or anywhere else. A CI step (`pnpm check:brands`) enforces this.

If you are a real-world brand owner and have concerns about the parody framework, please open
a GitHub Issue at the project repository — most concerns can be addressed by adjusting tone
or removing borderline entries from the registry.

## 5. Crests, kits, trophies.

Crest and trophy art in the default install is sourced exclusively from **Wikimedia
Commons** open-licensed vectors. Every asset is registered in `ATTRIBUTION.md` with its
source URL and license. **No paid asset CDN ever gets bundled.**

## 6. Player photographs and likenesses.

The UI uses names, flags, jersey numbers, and procedurally drawn stylized avatars. **No real
player face photograph or likeness is bundled or required.**

## 7. Audio.

SFX samples shipped in `assets/audio/` were generated by the **MiniMax audio model** under
project license. They are CC0-equivalent for use inside GoFoot. The game uses
`window.speechSynthesis` (Web Speech API) for live in-match narration — no third-party voice
service is invoked at runtime.

## 8. Brand assets.

`assets/logo.svg`, `assets/logo-horizontal.svg`, and `assets/favicon.svg` are original
artwork, MIT-licensed, drawn specifically for GoFoot. The G and F mark inside the emblem
is a stylized wordmark, not derived from any real brand.
